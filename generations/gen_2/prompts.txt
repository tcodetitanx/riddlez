GENERATION 2 - ARCHETYPE FUSION VARIANTS
=========================================
Base: Mutation E from Gen 1 (Archetype Fusion Novelty)
Strategy: Each mutation explores a specific unexplored fusion

================================================================================
MUTATION 2A: Bayesian + Self-Reference
================================================================================
"Create a puzzle where updating beliefs changes the thing you're reasoning about.
The act of observation or prediction must affect probabilities in a way that
requires considering your own impact on the system.

Examples:
- Your estimate of X affects X
- Learning information changes what's true
- The probability of your prediction being right depends on what you predict"

================================================================================
MUTATION 2B: Common Knowledge + Game Theory
================================================================================
"Create a puzzle where strategic behavior depends on what everyone knows everyone
knows. The optimal move changes based on levels of meta-knowledge.

Examples:
- A strategy only works if everyone knows it exists
- The announcement of a rule changes how people play
- Coordination requires common knowledge of some fact"

================================================================================
MUTATION 2C: State Space + Encoding
================================================================================
"Create a puzzle where navigating through states reveals or hides information.
The path you take encodes or decodes a message.

Examples:
- Your route through a maze spells out the answer
- Each move reveals one bit of information about the goal
- The constraints on movement encode a secret pattern"

================================================================================
MUTATION 2D: Constraint Satisfaction + Parity
================================================================================
"Create a puzzle where constraints involve parity (odd/even) in non-obvious ways.
The solution must satisfy both explicit constraints and hidden parity requirements.

Examples:
- Arrangements where odd/even positions matter
- Solutions that only work with certain count parities
- Constraints that secretly depend on parity of some property"

================================================================================
MUTATION 2E: Game Theory + Self-Reference (Double Down)
================================================================================
"Create a puzzle with strategic agents whose strategies reference themselves.
What you should do depends on what others do, which depends on what you do.

Examples:
- Players predicting each other's predictions of their predictions
- Strategies that only work if no one expects them
- Games where the rules depend on players' beliefs about the rules"

================================================================================
