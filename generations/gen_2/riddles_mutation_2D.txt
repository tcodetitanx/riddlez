RIDDLE 2D1: The Parity Prison
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
A prison has 100 cells numbered 1-100, arranged in a row. Each cell has a light
that's either ON or OFF. Initially, all lights are OFF.

A prisoner walks down the row. At cell N, they MUST toggle the light IF:
- N is even AND they've toggled an ODD number of lights so far, OR
- N is odd AND they've toggled an EVEN number of lights so far

THE QUESTION: After walking past all 100 cells, which lights are ON?

HINT: Track the parity of your toggle count as you go.

SOLUTION:
Step 1: Start at cell 1. Toggled so far: 0 (even).
        Cell 1 is odd. Rule: toggle if odd cell AND even count. Yes! Toggle cell 1.
        Count: 1 (odd).

Step 2: Cell 2. Count is odd.
        Cell 2 is even. Rule: toggle if even cell AND odd count. Yes! Toggle cell 2.
        Count: 2 (even).

Step 3: Cell 3. Count is even.
        Cell 3 is odd. Rule: toggle if odd cell AND even count. Yes! Toggle cell 3.
        Count: 3 (odd).

Step 4: Pattern emerging: at every cell, the condition is met!
        - At odd cell with even count: toggle (makes count odd).
        - At even cell with odd count: toggle (makes count even).

Step 5: The parity of count always matches the parity of the cell number after processing.
        So at cell N: we've toggled N-1 times, count = N-1.
        If N is odd: count N-1 is even. Condition met, toggle.
        If N is even: count N-1 is odd. Condition met, toggle.

Step 6: ALL 100 cells are toggled exactly once.

ANSWER: All 100 lights are ON.

VERIFICATION:
- Cell 1: toggle (odd cell, 0 even toggles). Light ON.
- Cell 2: toggle (even cell, 1 odd toggle). Light ON.
- Cell 3: toggle (odd cell, 2 even toggles). Light ON.
- ...
- Cell 100: toggle (even cell, 99 odd toggles). Light ON.
- All lights ON. ✓

RIDDLE 2D2: The Checkered Choices
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
Alice and Bob play a game on a 4×4 grid. They take turns placing tokens.
- Alice places white tokens on white squares (like a checkerboard).
- Bob places black tokens on black squares.
- Alice goes first.

A player wins if they complete a row, column, or diagonal of their color tokens.
Each player plays optimally.

THE QUESTION: Who wins?

HINT: Count the squares.

SOLUTION:
Step 1: 4×4 checkerboard: 8 white squares, 8 black squares.
Step 2: Alice places on white, Bob on black. They don't block each other!
Step 3: Wait - can a row/column/diagonal be all one color?

Step 4: Checkerboard coloring: adjacent squares are different colors.
        A row of 4: alternates W-B-W-B or B-W-B-W. No row is monochrome.
        Same for columns.

Step 5: Diagonals? The main diagonals (4 squares) alternate too.
        Short diagonals (2-3 squares) also alternate.

Step 6: THERE IS NO ROW, COLUMN, OR DIAGONAL OF ALL ONE COLOR on a checkerboard!

Step 7: Neither player can win. The game is a guaranteed draw (or infinite).

ANSWER: Neither wins. The game is a draw.

INSIGHT: The checkerboard parity makes all winning lines impossible.

VERIFICATION:
- Row 1: W-B-W-B. Can't be all white or all black. ✓
- Column 1: W-B-W-B. Same. ✓
- Main diagonal: W-B-W-B. Same. ✓
- No winning condition can be met. ✓

ARCHETYPE SCORES:
- Originality: 6/10 (checkerboard parity is known)
- Cleverness: 7/10 (the impossibility insight is clean)
- Simplicity: 9/10 (very clear setup)
- Solution Success: 10/10 (perfect)

RIDDLE 2D3: The Odd One Out
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
You have 9 coins arranged in a 3×3 grid. Each coin shows either HEADS or TAILS.
The initial configuration has an EVEN number of HEADS in every row, column, and
both main diagonals (8 lines total).

You must flip exactly ONE coin to make an ODD number of HEADS in every line.

THE QUESTION: Which coin can you flip?

HINT: The center coin is special.

SOLUTION:
Step 1: Each row has even heads. Each column has even heads. Both diagonals have even heads.
Step 2: Total heads in grid = sum of row heads = even + even + even = even.
Step 3: If we flip a coin from T to H: that row/column/diagonal gains 1 head (now odd).
        If we flip from H to T: that row/column/diagonal loses 1 head (now odd).

Step 4: The center coin is in: row 2, column 2, both diagonals. That's 4 lines.
        The corner coins are in: 1 row, 1 column, 1 diagonal. That's 3 lines.
        The edge coins are in: 1 row, 1 column. That's 2 lines.

Step 5: Flipping the center changes parity of 4 lines.
        Flipping a corner changes parity of 3 lines.
        Flipping an edge changes parity of 2 lines.

Step 6: We need to change ALL 8 lines from even to odd.
        One flip can change at most 4 lines (center).
        We need to change 8 lines with 1 flip? IMPOSSIBLE.

Step 7: Wait, let me recount. There are 8 lines: 3 rows + 3 columns + 2 diagonals.
        Each coin is in exactly:
        - Center: 1 row + 1 col + 2 diag = 4 lines
        - Corner: 1 row + 1 col + 1 diag = 3 lines
        - Edge: 1 row + 1 col + 0 diag = 2 lines

Step 8: To change all 8 from even to odd, we need to flip parity of all 8.
        But any single flip changes at most 4. Impossible!

ANSWER: It's IMPOSSIBLE to flip exactly one coin to make all lines odd.

WAIT - let me verify the premise. Is the initial state possible?

Step 9: Every row even, every column even. Let H be the 3×3 matrix of heads (1) and tails (0).
        Sum of each row = even. Sum of each column = even.
        Sum of matrix = even (sum of row sums).
        Diagonals: for BOTH diagonals to be even while rows/cols are even... is this possible?

        Example: All zeros (all tails). Every line is 0 (even). ✓
        In this case, flipping ANY coin makes that coin's lines odd (one flip, not all 8).

        The puzzle asks to make ALL 8 lines odd with one flip. But one flip affects
        2-4 lines, not all 8.

FINAL ANSWER: Impossible. No single flip can change all 8 lines from even to odd.
