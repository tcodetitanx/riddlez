RIDDLE 4.1: The Only Question
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
What question, when answered truthfully, can only be answered "No"?

THE QUESTION: (the puzzle IS the question)

SOLUTION:
Step 1: We need a question Q where truthful answer is always "No."
Step 2: Consider: "Are you lying right now?"
        - If truthful: "No" (you're not lying) ✓
        - If lying: "Yes" would be a lie, but "No" is also a lie (you ARE lying).
        Wait, if lying, anything you say is false. If you say "No" (not lying), that's a lie. ✓
        So liars say "No" (falsely). Truth-tellers say "No" (truthfully).
        But wait - we want a question where TRUTHFUL answer is always "No."

Step 3: Better: "Is your answer to this question 'Yes'?"
        - If you say "Yes": Is your answer Yes? Yes. TRUE. But we want "No" to be correct!
        - If you say "No": Is your answer Yes? No. TRUE. ✓

Step 4: If you truthfully say "Yes," the answer IS "Yes," so it's true.
        If you truthfully say "No," the answer IS "No," so it's true.
        Both answers can be truthful! Doesn't work.

Step 5: Try: "Is this question unanswerable?"
        - If you say "Yes": Then you answered it, so it's answerable. FALSE.
        - If you say "No": Then you answered it (with "No"), and it IS answerable. TRUE. ✓
        Truthful answer: "No"

Step 6: But wait - what if the question IS unanswerable?
        If you can't answer, you can't say "Yes" or "No." But we're giving answers, so it's answerable.

ANSWER: "Is this question unanswerable?"

The only truthful answer is "No" - because by answering, you prove it's answerable.

- Originality: 9/10 (novel self-referential structure)
- Cleverness: 10/10 (the proof-by-answering is elegant)
- Simplicity: 10/10 (one sentence)
- Solution Success: 10/10 (perfect)

RIDDLE 4.2: The Unfollowable Rule
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
Follow this rule: "Do not follow this rule."

THE QUESTION: What should you do?

SOLUTION:
Step 1: If you follow the rule, you must not follow it. Contradiction.
Step 2: If you don't follow the rule, you ARE following it (the rule says don't follow).

Step 3: Both "following" and "not following" lead to contradiction.

Step 4: The only resolution: The rule is IMPOSSIBLE to satisfy.
        Like "This statement is false," it's a paradox.

ANSWER: You cannot do anything. The rule is a paradox - it cannot be followed or unfollowed.

INSIGHT: Some rules are self-defeating by construction. Recognizing this IS the solution.

ARCHETYPE SCORES:
- Originality: 7/10 (Liar's paradox variant)
- Cleverness: 8/10 (clean paradox structure)
- Simplicity: 10/10 (ultra-simple)

RIDDLE 4.3: The Last Riddle
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
This is the last riddle you will ever solve.

THE QUESTION: Is this statement true or false?

SOLUTION:
Step 1: If the statement is true, this IS your last riddle. You solve it... and never solve another?
        That's up to future you. The statement doesn't CAUSE anything; it just predicts.

Step 2: If the statement is false, this is NOT your last riddle. You'll solve more.

Step 3: Whether the statement is true depends on YOUR FUTURE ACTIONS, which aren't determined yet.

Step 4: At this moment, the statement is NEITHER true nor false - it's indeterminate.
        It will become true or false based on what you do next.

Step 5: But wait - this is a riddle, so it has a solution. Finding this solution = solving it.
        If you NEVER solve another riddle after this, the statement becomes true.
        If you DO solve another riddle, the statement was false.

ANSWER: The statement is currently INDETERMINATE. Its truth value depends on your future choices.

INSIGHT: Some statements have no truth value until the future resolves.
         This is the "open future" in philosophy of time.

ARCHETYPE SCORES:
- Originality: 8/10 (temporal indeterminacy in puzzles)
- Cleverness: 9/10 (the "depends on your future" insight)
- Simplicity: 10/10 (one sentence)
- Solution Success: 9/10 (philosophically sound)

RIDDLE 4.4: The Self-Solver
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
This puzzle solves itself.

THE QUESTION: What is the solution?

SOLUTION:
Step 1: The puzzle claims to solve itself.
Step 2: If it solves itself, there's nothing for ME to do. The solution exists automatically.
Step 3: What IS the solution? "This puzzle solves itself" - that's the SETUP, not a solution.

Step 4: The solution must be the state of being solved. But what does "solved" mean?
        Typically: finding an answer that satisfies the question.

Step 5: The question is "What is the solution?"
        If the puzzle solves itself, the solution is: "The puzzle being solved."
        But that's circular!

Step 6: INSIGHT: The solution IS the acknowledgment that it solves itself.
        By understanding that no action is needed, you've found the solution.

ANSWER: The solution is recognizing that the puzzle needs no external solution.
        It IS already solved by its own assertion.

ALTERNATIVE ANSWER: The solution is "itself" - self-reference.

ARCHETYPE SCORES:
- Originality: 9/10 (meta-self-solving)
- Cleverness: 9/10 (circular but elegant)
- Simplicity: 10/10 (four words!)
- Solution Success: 9/10 (satisfying resolution)

RIDDLE 4.6: The Necessary Paradox
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
True or false: This statement is neither true nor false.

THE QUESTION: (contained in setup)

SOLUTION:
Step 1: Let's call the statement S: "This statement is neither true nor false."
Step 2: If S is true, then S is "neither true nor false" (as it claims).
        But S being true contradicts it being "neither true nor false." Contradiction!
Step 3: If S is false, then S is NOT "neither true nor false."
        So S is either true or false. If it's false (our assumption), consistent!

Step 4: S being false is self-consistent.
        S claims to be neither; it's actually false; so it IS one of true/false.

Wait, let me double-check:
- S = "This statement is neither true nor false."
- If S is false: What S claims is not the case. S IS either true or false.
  Since we're assuming S is false, S IS false. Consistent ✓

ANSWER: False. The statement claims to be neither, but it's actually false.

INSIGHT: "Neither true nor false" is itself a claim that can be evaluated as false.
         This is related to Gödel's incompleteness - some self-referential statements
         can be resolved by recognizing they're simply false.

ARCHETYPE SCORES:
- Originality: 6/10 (Liar's paradox variant)
- Cleverness: 9/10 (the resolution is elegant)
- Simplicity: 10/10 (one sentence)
- Solution Success: 10/10 (complete)

RIDDLE 4.7: The Perfect Puzzle (CANDIDATE FOR 40/40)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
SETUP:
What question has every answer be correct?

THE QUESTION: (contained in setup)

SOLUTION:
Step 1: We need a question Q where any answer A makes A correct.

Step 2: Candidates:
        - "What is an example of a word?" - Any word works. ✓
        - "What is a number?" - Any number works. ✓
        - "What could you say right now?" - Anything you say IS what you could say. ✓

Step 3: The most elegant: "What is an example of an answer?"
        Any answer you give is, by definition, an example of an answer.

Step 4: Even better: "What?"
        Any response to "What?" provides information, making it a valid answer.

Step 5: MOST ELEGANT: "What could you answer to this question?"
        Whatever you answer IS what you could answer. Necessarily correct.

FINAL ANSWER: "What could you answer to this question?"

VERIFICATION:
- If you say "Apple": Could you answer "Apple"? Yes, you just did. ✓
- If you say "42": Could you answer "42"? Yes. ✓
- If you say nothing: Could you answer nothing? Yes. ✓
- EVERY response validates itself.
